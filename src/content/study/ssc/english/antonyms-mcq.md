Antonyms are words that have opposite meanings. This is an important topic in SSC CHSL English Language section, where you need to identify the word most opposite in meaning to the given word. Practice these carefully curated questions to strengthen your vocabulary.

## Practice Questions

### Question 1

**Select the most appropriate ANTONYM of the given word.**

**Stingy**

1. Generous
2. Humorous
3. Lascivious
4. Deceitful

<details>
<summary><strong>Show Answer</strong></summary>

**Answer: 1. Generous**

**Explanation:**
- **Stingy** means unwilling to spend money or give things; miserly, tight-fisted
- **Generous** means willing to give and share; liberal, open-handed
- These words are direct antonyms
- Other options:
  - Humorous = funny, amusing (unrelated)
  - Lascivious = lustful (unrelated)
  - Deceitful = dishonest (unrelated)

</details>

**Shame**

1. Disgrace
2. Defamation
3. Pride
4. Fear

<details>
<summary><strong>Show Answer</strong></summary>

**Answer: 3. Pride**

**Explanation:**

- These are opposite emotions

  - Clever = intelligent (unrelated to courage)

### Question 4

**Select the most appropriate ANTONYM of the bold word in the given sentence.**

*It is no exaggeration to say the Hussain Sagar Lake is an **integral** element of Hyderabad and Hyderabad's history.*

1. essential
2. total
3. extra
4. inherent

<details>
<summary><strong>Show Answer</strong></summary>

**Answer: 3. extra**

**Explanation:**
- **Integral** means necessary to make a whole complete; essential, fundamental
- **Extra** means added to an existing amount; additional, supplementary, not essential
- In context, integral means the lake is a fundamental part, while extra would mean it's not necessary
- Other options:
  - Essential = absolutely necessary (synonym)
  - Total = complete (related but not opposite)
  - Inherent = existing as a natural part (synonym)

</details>

1. co-operates

4. intermingles

<details>

**Answer: 3. disconnects**

## Common Prefixes for Antonyms

Understanding prefixes can help you identify antonyms quickly:

- **un-**: happy → unhappy, able → unable
- **in-**: correct → incorrect, visible → invisible
- **im-**: possible → impossible, mature → immature
- **il-**: legal → illegal, logical → illogical
- **ir-**: regular → irregular, responsible → irresponsible
- **dis-**: agree → disagree, honest → dishonest
- **mis-**: understand → misunderstand, lead → mislead
- **non-**: sense → nonsense, violent → nonviolent
