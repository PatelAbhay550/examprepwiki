# Structure of Indian Banking System

The Indian Banking System is one of the oldest and most developed banking systems in the world. It has evolved through various phases and currently consists of a well-structured hierarchy.

## Overview of Banking Structure

The Indian Banking System can be broadly classified into:

1. **Scheduled Banks**
   - Commercial Banks
   - Cooperative Banks

2. **Non-Scheduled Banks**
   - Local Area Banks
   - Payment Banks
   - Small Finance Banks

## Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

**Established:** April 1, 1935 (under RBI Act, 1934)

**Headquarters:** Mumbai

**Current Governor:** Shaktikanta Das (as of 2025)

### Functions of RBI

1. **Monetary Authority:** Formulates and implements monetary policy
2. **Regulator:** Regulates banking and financial system
3. **Supervisor:** Supervises and monitors banking operations
4. **Manager of Foreign Exchange:** Manages foreign exchange reserves
5. **Issuer of Currency:** Sole authority to issue currency notes (except ₹1 note and coins)
6. **Banker to Banks:** Acts as lender of last resort
7. **Banker to Government:** Manages government accounts and debt

### Key Policy Rates (November 2025)

| Rate | Current Value | Purpose |
|------|---------------|---------|
| Repo Rate | 6.50% | Rate at which RBI lends to banks |
| Reverse Repo Rate | 3.35% | Rate at which RBI borrows from banks |
| CRR | 4.50% | Cash Reserve Ratio |
| SLR | 18.00% | Statutory Liquidity Ratio |
| Bank Rate | 6.75% | Long-term lending rate |
| MSF | 6.75% | Marginal Standing Facility |

## Commercial Banks

Commercial banks are the backbone of the Indian financial system, providing various banking services to individuals, businesses, and governments.

### Types of Commercial Banks

#### 1. Public Sector Banks (PSBs)

**Definition:** Banks where majority stake (more than 50%) is held by the government.

**Major PSBs (Post-Consolidation):**
- State Bank of India (SBI)
- Punjab National Bank (PNB)
- Bank of Baroda (BoB)
- Canara Bank
- Union Bank of India
- Indian Bank
- Bank of India
- Central Bank of India
- Indian Overseas Bank
- UCO Bank
- Bank of Maharashtra
- Punjab & Sind Bank

**Number of PSBs:** 12 (as of 2025)

#### 2. Private Sector Banks

**Definition:** Banks where majority stake is held by private entities.

**Major Private Banks:**
- HDFC Bank
- ICICI Bank
- Axis Bank
- Kotak Mahindra Bank
- IndusInd Bank
- Yes Bank
- IDFC First Bank
- Bandhan Bank

#### 3. Foreign Banks

**Definition:** Banks headquartered in foreign countries but operating in India.

**Examples:**
- Citibank
- Standard Chartered Bank
- HSBC
- Deutsche Bank
- DBS Bank

#### 4. Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)

**Established:** 1975 (based on Narasimham Committee recommendations)

**Ownership Structure:**
- Central Government: 50%
- State Government: 15%
- Sponsor Bank: 35%

**Purpose:** Provide credit to rural areas, especially agriculture and small industries

**Number of RRBs:** 43 (as of 2025)

## Cooperative Banks

These banks operate on cooperative principles and cater primarily to rural areas and agriculture.

### Structure of Cooperative Banking

**Three-Tier Structure:**

1. **Primary Agricultural Credit Societies (PACS)** - Village level
2. **District Central Cooperative Banks (DCCBs)** - District level
3. **State Cooperative Banks (SCBs)** - State level

### Urban Cooperative Banks (UCBs)

These operate in urban and semi-urban areas providing banking services to small businesses and middle-income groups.

## New Age Banks

### Payment Banks

**Launched:** 2015

**Key Features:**
- Cannot lend or issue credit cards
- Can accept deposits up to ₹2 lakh per customer
- Can offer remittance and payment services
- Must maintain CRR and SLR

**Examples:**
- Paytm Payments Bank
- India Post Payments Bank
- Airtel Payments Bank
- Fino Payments Bank

### Small Finance Banks

**Launched:** 2015

**Objective:** Financial inclusion - provide banking to unserved and underserved sections

**Key Features:**
- Can provide full banking services
- Focus on small business units, small and marginal farmers, micro and small industries
- 75% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC) must be given as priority sector lending

**Examples:**
- AU Small Finance Bank
- Ujjivan Small Finance Bank
- Equitas Small Finance Bank
- Jana Small Finance Bank

## Banking Regulations and Reforms

### Basel Norms

**Basel I (1988):** Focus on credit risk, minimum capital requirement of 8%

**Basel II (2004):** Introduced three pillars:
1. Minimum capital requirements
2. Supervisory review
3. Market discipline

**Basel III (2010):** Stricter capital requirements, introduced leverage ratio and liquidity coverage ratio

**Implementation in India:** Being implemented in phases, expected full compliance by 2025

### Recent Banking Reforms

1. **Bank Consolidation (2017-2020):**
   - Reduced PSBs from 27 to 12
   - Created larger, globally competitive banks

2. **National Asset Reconstruction Company Ltd (NARCL):**
   - Established to resolve stressed assets
   - Also known as "Bad Bank"

3. **Digital Banking:**
   - Promotion of UPI, IMPS, RTGS, NEFT
   - Digital lending platforms
   - Open Banking initiatives

4. **Financial Inclusion:**
   - Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)
   - Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)
   - Atal Pension Yojana

## Important Banking Terms

**CASA Ratio:** Current Account and Savings Account ratio - indicates low-cost deposits

**NPA (Non-Performing Asset):** Loan where interest/principal payment is overdue for 90 days

**Gross NPA:** Total NPAs / Total Advances

**Net NPA:** (Gross NPA - Provisions) / (Total Advances - Provisions)

**Priority Sector Lending:** Lending to sectors considered priority by government (agriculture, MSMEs, education, housing)

**Core Banking Solution (CBS):** Centralized banking system allowing 'anywhere banking'
