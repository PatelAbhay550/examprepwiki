Ancient India created a huge library of writings on many different subjects.

These writings covered:

- Arts: Drama and poetry.

- Science: Mathematics, astronomy, and medicine.

- Society: Politics and philosophy (deep thinking about life).

These works show just how brilliant ancient Indian civilization was, and their ideas are still important and influential in the modern world. This article covers important books written by Authors of Ancient India.

## Overview

Sanskrit literature flourished during different periods of ancient Indian history, particularly during the Gupta Period (320-550 CE), often called the "Golden Age of India." Writers and scholars made significant contributions to various branches of knowledge, creating timeless masterpieces that have been studied for centuries.

## Classification of Ancient Indian Literature

Ancient Indian literary works can be broadly classified into:

1. **Religious and Philosophical Texts**: Vedas, Upanishads, Puranas
2. **Epic Literature**: Ramayana, Mahabharata
3. **Sanskrit Drama and Poetry**: Works of Kalidasa, Bhasa, etc.
4. **Scientific Treatises**: Mathematics, Astronomy, Medicine
5. **Political and Administrative Works**: Arthashastra, Nitishastra
6. **Grammatical Works**: Ashtadhyayi by Panini

## Detailed Description of Major Works

### 1. Literature and Drama

#### Kalidasa - The Greatest Sanskrit Poet
**Period**: 4th-5th century CE (Gupta Period)

Kalidasa is considered the greatest poet and dramatist in Sanskrit literature. His major works include:

- **Abhijnanashakuntalam**: A seven-act play about King Dushyanta and Shakuntala
- **Vikramorvashiya**: Drama about King Pururavas and celestial nymph Urvashi
- **Malavikagnimitram**: Comedy about King Agnimitra
- **Raghuvamsham**: Epic poem describing the dynasty of Raghu
- **Kumarasambhavam**: Epic poem about the birth of Kartikeya
- **Meghaduta**: Lyric poem about a yaksha sending a message via a cloud
- **Ritusamhara**: Poem describing the six seasons

#### Bhasa
**Period**: 2nd-3rd century CE

One of the earliest known Sanskrit dramatists. Notable works:
- **Swapnavasavadatta**: Dream of Vasavadatta
- **Pratijnayaugandharayana**: The vow of Yaugandharayana
- **Pancharatra**: Five nights

#### Shudraka
**Period**: 5th century CE

- **Mrichchhakatika** (The Little Clay Cart): Social drama depicting life in ancient India

#### Vishakhadatta
**Period**: 6th century CE

- **Mudrarakshasa**: Political drama about Chanakya's strategies

### 2. Historical Works

#### Kalhana
**Period**: 12th century CE

- **Rajatarangini**: Chronicle of the kings of Kashmir, first historical work in Sanskrit

#### Banabhatta
**Period**: 7th century CE

Court poet of King Harsha Vardhana:
- **Harshacharita**: Biography of King Harsha


### 3. Fables and Stories

#### Vishnu Sharma
**Period**: 3rd century BCE (approximately)

- **Panchatantra**: Collection of animal fables teaching political wisdom and life lessons

#### Somdeva
**Period**: 11th century CE

- **Kathasaritsagara** (Ocean of Stories): Largest collection of Indian legends and fairy tales

### 4. Scientific and Mathematical Works

#### Aryabhata
**Period**: 476-550 CE

Indian mathematician and astronomer:
- **Aryabhatiya**: Mathematical and astronomical treatise
- Introduced the concept of zero
- Calculated value of Ï€ (pi)
- Explained solar and lunar eclipses

#### Varahamihira
**Period**: 6th century CE

Astronomer, mathematician, and astrologer:
- **Brihat Samhita**: Encyclopedia covering astronomy, astrology, and natural phenomena
- **Panchsiddhantika**: Summary of five astronomical systems

#### Bhaskara II (Bhaskaracharya)
**Period**: 12th century CE

Mathematician and astronomer:
- **Lilavati**: Mathematics (arithmetic, algebra, geometry)
- **Bijaganita**: Algebra
- **Siddhanta Shiromani**: Astronomy

### 5. Medical Sciences

#### Charaka
**Period**: 2nd century CE

- **Charaka Samhita**: Foundational text of Ayurveda dealing with internal medicine

#### Sushruta
**Period**: 6th century BCE

- **Sushruta Samhita**: Ancient text on surgery and medicine

### 6. Grammar and Linguistics

#### Panini
**Period**: 6th-5th century BCE

- **Ashtadhyayi**: Comprehensive grammar of Sanskrit in eight chapters
- Foundation of linguistic analysis

#### Patanjali
**Period**: 2nd century BCE

- **Mahabhashya**: Commentary on Panini's Ashtadhyayi
- **Yoga Sutras**: Foundational text of Yoga philosophy

### 7. Political Science and Economics

#### Kautilya (Chanakya)
**Period**: 4th century BCE

Prime Minister of Chandragupta Maurya:
- **Arthashastra**: Treatise on statecraft, economic policy, and military strategy

#### Kamandaka
**Period**: 8th century CE

- **Nitisara**: Political ethics based on Arthashastra

### 8. Philosophy and Religion

#### Ashvaghosa
**Period**: 1st-2nd century CE

Buddhist philosopher and poet:
- **Buddhacharita**: Biography of Buddha
- **Saundarananda**: Buddhist philosophical poem

### 9. Art and Aesthetics

#### Bharata Muni
**Period**: 2nd century BCE - 2nd century CE

- **Natyashastra**: Ancient treatise on performing arts, drama, dance, and music

### 10. Love and Relationships

#### Vatsyayana
**Period**: 3rd century CE

- **Kamasutra**: Ancient text on human sexual behavior and love

## Key Points to Remember

### For Quick Revision

1. **Kalidasa** = Abhijnana Shakuntala, Meghaduta, Raghuvamsam
2. **Panini** = Ashtadhyayi (Sanskrit Grammar)
3. **Kautilya** = Arthashastra (Political Science)
4. **Aryabhata** = Aryabhatiya (Mathematics and Astronomy)
5. **Charaka** = Charaka Samhita (Ayurveda)
6. **Kalhana** = Rajatarangini (History of Kashmir)
7. **Vishnu Sharma** = Panchatantra (Fables)
8. **Bharata** = Natyashastra (Performing Arts)

### Famous Literary Figures by Period

**Mauryan Period (322-185 BCE)**:
- Kautilya (Arthashastra)
- Ashvaghosa (Buddhacharita)

**Gupta Period (320-550 CE)** - Golden Age:
- Kalidasa (Drama and Poetry)
- Aryabhata (Mathematics)
- Varahamihira (Astronomy)

**Post-Gupta Period**:
- Banabhatta (7th century)
- Bhaskara II (12th century)
- Kalhana (12th century)

## Practice Questions

1. Who wrote the Arthashastra?
   - **Answer**: Kautilya (Chanakya) wrote the Arthashastra.

2. Name the famous work of Kalidasa that describes the love story of Dushyanta and Shakuntala.
   - **Answer**: Abhijnana Shakuntalam was written by Kalidasa.

3. Which book is considered the first historical chronicle written in Sanskrit?
   - **Answer**: Rajatarangini by Kalhana is considered the first historical chronicle written in Sanskrit.

4. Who is the author of Panchatantra?
   - **Answer**: Vishnu Sharma is the author of Panchatantra.

5. Which mathematician introduced the concept of zero?
   - **Answer**: Aryabhata, a Gupta Era Indian mathematician, introduced the concept of zero.
