question: "Which article of the Constitution provides for Right to Education?"
options:
  - "Article 19"
  - "Article 21"
  - "Article 21A"
  - "Article 45"
correctAnswer: 2
explanation: "Article 21A provides the Right to Education as a Fundamental Right. It was inserted by the 86th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2002. It states that 'The State shall provide free and compulsory education to all children of the age of six to fourteen years in such manner as the State may, by law, determine.' This right is implemented through the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009. Note that Article 21 is Right to Life and Personal Liberty, and Article 45 (a DPSP) was amended to provide for early childhood care."
