question: "Which of the following is NOT a Fundamental Right under the Indian Constitution?"
options:
  - "Right to Equality"
  - "Right to Property"
  - "Right to Freedom of Religion"
  - "Right to Constitutional Remedies"
correctAnswer: 1
explanation: "The Right to Property was originally a Fundamental Right under Article 31. However, it was removed from the list of Fundamental Rights by the 44th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1978. It is now a Constitutional Right under Article 300A but not a Fundamental Right. Therefore, it cannot be enforced through Article 32 (Right to Constitutional Remedies)."
