## Overview

Egypt has officially opened the **Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)**, the world's largest archaeological museum dedicated to a single civilization. This monumental project, located near the iconic Giza pyramids, marks a significant milestone in preserving and showcasing ancient Egyptian heritage.

## Key Facts

### Museum Details

| **Aspect** | **Details** |
|------------|-------------|
| **Name** | Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) |
| **Location** | Giza Plateau, 2 km from Giza pyramids, 8 km from Cairo |
| **Cost** | Approximately $1.2 billion USD |
| **Construction Period** | Two decades (2005-2025) |
| **Total Artifacts** | Over 100,000 pieces |
| **Coverage** | 30 dynasties of ancient Egypt |
| **Inauguration Date** | November 1, 2025 |
| **Public Opening** | November 4, 2025 |

### Central Attraction

The **complete collection from King Tutankhamun's tomb** is the museum's central attraction, while other artifacts like **colossal statue of Ramses II**, and the **Khufu Solar Boat** are also prominently displayed. This marks the **first time since 1922** (when the tomb was discovered by Howard Carter) that the entire collection is displayed together in one location.

## Inauguration Ceremony

### November 1, 2025 - Official Opening

- **Attendance:** Leaders and dignitaries from over 70 countries
- **Special Features:** 
  - Pharaonic-themed light show
  - Drone show spectacular
  - Cultural performances celebrating ancient Egyptian civilization
- **Significance:** One of the most attended cultural events of 2025

### November 4, 2025 - Public Opening

The museum opened its doors to the general public three days after the official inauguration ceremony.

## Museum Features

### Architecture & Design

- **Strategic Location:** Positioned on the edge of the Giza Plateau with views of the pyramids
- **Modern Facilities:** World-class infrastructure with climate control and advanced conservation technology
- **Visitor Capacity:** Designed to accommodate large numbers of tourists with modern amenities

### Collection Highlights

1. **King Tutankhamun Collection**
   - Complete tomb artifacts
   - Gold funerary mask
   - Chariots, jewelry, and royal regalia
   - Personal items of the young pharaoh
   - First complete display since discovery

2. **Artifacts from 30 Dynasties**
   - Spanning over 3,000 years of Egyptian history
   - Old Kingdom, Middle Kingdom, and New Kingdom artifacts
   - Ptolemaic and Roman period pieces
   - Royal mummies and sarcophagi

3. **Over 100,000 Objects**
   - Largest collection of Egyptian antiquities in the world
   - Many items never before displayed publicly
   - Comprehensive representation of ancient Egyptian civilization

4. **Special Exhibits**
   - Colossal statue of Ramses II
   - Khufu Solar Boat
   - Interactive displays and educational installations

## Historical Context

### Ancient Egyptian Civilization

- **Timeline:** Approximately 3100 BCE - 30 BCE
- **Dynasties:** 30 dynastic periods plus Ptolemaic era
- **Major Periods:**
  - Old Kingdom (Pyramid Age)
  - Middle Kingdom
  - New Kingdom (Imperial Age)
  - Late Period
  - Ptolemaic Period

### King Tutankhamun

- **Reign:** Approximately 1332-1323 BCE (New Kingdom, 18th Dynasty)
- **Significance:** While a minor pharaoh during his lifetime, his tomb's discovery in 1922 by Howard Carter revolutionized Egyptology
- **Tomb Discovery:** Valley of the Kings, 1922 - most intact royal tomb ever found
- **Age at Death:** Approximately 19 years old
