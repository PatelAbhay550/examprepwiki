## Question: Who wrote the National Anthem of India?

### Short Answer
**Rabindranath Tagore** wrote the National Anthem of India, "Jana Gana Mana."

### Detailed Explanation

"Jana Gana Mana" is India's National Anthem, a powerful composition that celebrates India's diversity and unity, written by Nobel Laureate Rabindranath Tagore.

#### Key Details

**Basic Information:**
- **Title**: Jana Gana Mana (meaning: "Thou Art the Ruler of All Minds")
- **Author & Composer**: Rabindranath Tagore
- **Original Language**: Bengali (Sanskritized Bengali)
- **Original Title**: "Bharoto Bhagyo Bidhata" (Dispenser of India's Destiny)
- **First Sung**: December 27, 1911
- **Officially Adopted**: January 24, 1950
- **Duration**: Approximately 52 seconds (full version)

#### History and Composition

**1. Creation (1911)**
- Written by Tagore in December 1911
- Original Bengali version titled "Bharoto Bhagyo Bidhata"
- Part of Tagore's larger collection of patriotic songs
- Composed at his Shantiniketan residence

**2. First Performance (December 27, 1911)**
- Sung at the Calcutta Session of Indian National Congress
- Venue: Calcutta (now Kolkata)
- Second day of the session
- Initially not recognized as the national anthem

**3. Translation (1919)**
- Tagore himself translated it into English
- Title: "Morning Song of India"
- Published in English with the title "The Morning Song of India"

**4. Official Adoption (1950)**
- January 24, 1950: Adopted by Constituent Assembly
- Two days before Republic Day (January 26, 1950)
- Chosen over "Vande Mataram" (another patriotic song)

#### The Complete Text

**First Stanza (Official National Anthem):**

```
Jana-gana-mana-adhinayaka jaya he
Bharata-bhagya-vidhata
Punjab-Sindh-Gujarat-Maratha
Dravida-Utkala-Banga
Vindhya-Himachala-Yamuna-Ganga
Uchchala-jaladhi-taranga

Tava shubha name jage
Tava shubha asisa mage
Gahe tava jaya-gatha
Jana-gana-mangala-dayaka jaya he
Bharata-bhagya-vidhata
Jaya he, jaya he, jaya he
Jaya jaya jaya jaya he!
```

**English Translation:**
"Thou art the ruler of the minds of all people,
Dispenser of India's destiny.
Thy name rouses the hearts of Punjab, Sind,
Gujarat and Maratha,
Of the Dravid and Orissa and Bengal;
It echoes in the hills of the Vindhyas and Himalayas,
mingles in the music of Yamuna and Ganga
and is chanted by the waves of the Indian Ocean.
They pray for thy blessings and sing thy praise.
The salvation of all people is in thy hand,
Thou dispenser of India's destiny.
Victory, victory, victory to thee."

#### Important Facts

**Structure:**
- Total stanzas in complete song: 5
- National Anthem uses: Only first stanza
- Playing time (full): 52 seconds
- Playing time (short): 20 seconds (permitted in certain circumstances)

**Regions Mentioned:**
- Punjab, Sindh, Gujarat, Maratha (Western India)
- Dravida (Southern India)
- Utkala/Orissa (Eastern India)
- Banga/Bengal (Eastern India)
- Vindhya, Himalayas (Mountain ranges)
- Yamuna, Ganga (Rivers)
- Indian Ocean (Seas)

#### Rabindranath Tagore (1861-1941)

**Brief Biography:**
- First non-European to win Nobel Prize in Literature (1913)
- For his collection "Gitanjali" (Song Offerings)
- Polymath: Poet, writer, composer, philosopher, artist
- Founded Visva-Bharati University at Shantiniketan

**Other National Contributions:**
- **India**: National Anthem "Jana Gana Mana"
- **Bangladesh**: National Anthem "Amar Sonar Bangla"
- **Sri Lanka**: Influenced national anthem
- **Only person** associated with national anthems of three nations

**When to Stand:**
- During playing or singing of National Anthem
- Everyone present must stand in attention
- Conversations must stop

**Occasions:**
- National and state functions
- Schools and educational institutions

#### Common Controversy

**Was it written for King George V?**
- **FALSE**: Common misconception
- Tagore clarified it was for "Bharata Bhagya Vidhata" (God/Eternal Charioteer of India)
- Written for the Calcutta Session of INC
- King George V visited India in December 1911 (coincidental timing)
- Tagore explicitly denied it was for the British King

#### Vande Mataram vs Jana Gana Mana

**Why Jana Gana Mana was chosen:**

| Aspect | Jana Gana Mana | Vande Mataram |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| **Inclusivity** | Secular, represents all regions | Religious imagery (controversial for some) |
| **Language** | Sanskritized Bengali | Sanskrit |
| **Acceptance** | Universal acceptance | Some groups found it objectionable |
| **Status** | National Anthem | National Song |
