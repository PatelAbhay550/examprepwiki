## Question: Who wrote 'The Discovery of India'?

### Short Answer
**Jawaharlal Nehru** wrote 'The Discovery of India'.

### Detailed Explanation

'The Discovery of India' is one of the most significant works on Indian history, culture, and philosophy written during the Indian independence movement.

#### Book Details

1. **Author**: Jawaharlal Nehru (India's first Prime Minister)
2. **Written**: April 1942 - March 1946
3. **Location**: Ahmednagar Fort Prison, Maharashtra
4. **Published**: 1946
5. **Publisher**: Penguin Books
6. **Pages**: 595
7. **Language**: English

#### Context of Writing

**Imprisonment During Quit India Movement**
- Nehru was arrested on August 9, 1942
- Spent nearly 3 years in Ahmednagar Fort
- Used this time for deep introspection and writing
- No access to extensive reference materials
- Wrote from memory and limited books available

#### Major Themes Covered

1. **Ancient India**
   - Indus Valley Civilization
   - Vedic period and philosophy
   - Buddhist and Jain periods
   - Classical age achievements

2. **Medieval India**
   - Islamic invasions and rule
   - Cultural synthesis
   - Bhakti and Sufi movements

3. **Colonial Period**
   - British East India Company
   - 1857 Revolt
   - Colonial exploitation
   - Economic impact

4. **Freedom Struggle**
   - Rise of nationalism
   - Gandhian movement
   - Role of various leaders
   - Vision for independent India

5. **Philosophy and Culture**
   - Indian philosophy and thought
   - Unity in diversity
   - Syncretism in Indian culture
   - Scientific and artistic achievements

#### Significance

- **Historical Document**: First-person account of India's struggle
- **Cultural Analysis**: Deep dive into Indian civilization
- **Personal Reflection**: Nehru's vision for modern India
- **Literary Merit**: Eloquent prose and historical narrative
- **Educational Value**: Comprehensive overview of Indian history

#### Other Books by Nehru

1. **"Glimpses of World History" (1934)**
   - Written in Naini Jail
   - 196 letters to daughter Indira
   - Survey of world history

2. **"An Autobiography" (1936)**
   - Also called "Toward Freedom"
   - Personal and political journey

3. **"Letters from a Father to His Daughter" (1929)**
   - 30 letters to young Indira Gandhi
   - Science and history for children
