## Question: Who was the first woman to climb Mount Everest?

### Short Answer
**Junko Tabei** from Japan was the first woman to climb Mount Everest on **May 16, 1975**.

#### The Historic Expedition

1. **Date of Summit**: May 16, 1975
2. **Expedition**: Japanese Women's Everest Expedition (15 women team)
3. **Age**: 35 years old
4. **Route**: Southeast Ridge (Nepal side)
5. **Sherpa Guide**: Ang Tsering

#### Challenges Overcome

1. **Avalanche Disaster**
   - 12 days before summit, an avalanche struck their camp
   - Tabei was buried under snow and had to be rescued
   - Suffered injuries but continued the expedition

2. **Social Barriers**
   - Faced criticism in conservative Japanese society
   - Struggled to find sponsors (companies doubted women climbers)
   - Had to balance motherhood with mountaineering

3. **Physical Challenges**
   - Small stature (4'9" / 145 cm)
   - Harsh Himalayan conditions
   - Limited resources compared to male expeditions

#### Further Achievements

- **1992**: First woman to complete the Seven Summits
- **Climbed**: 70+ mountains worldwide
- **Environmental Work**: Promoted "Leave No Trace" mountaineering
- **Education**: Organized cleanups on Mount Everest
- **Author**: Wrote extensively about her experiences

#### Legacy and Recognition

- **Honorary Degrees**: Multiple universities worldwide
- **Awards**: Japanese Medal of Honor, numerous international recognitions
- **Inspiration**: Opened doors for women in adventure sports
- **Death**: October 20, 2016 (age 77) - continued climbing until shortly before
