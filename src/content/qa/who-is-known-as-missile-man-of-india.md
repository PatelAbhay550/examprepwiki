## Question: Who is known as the Missile Man of India?

### Short Answer
**Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam** (Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) is known as the Missile Man of India.

### Detailed Explanation

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam earned the title "Missile Man of India" for his extraordinary contributions to India's defense and space programs, particularly in missile technology development.

#### Why "Missile Man"?

**Chief Architect of India's Missile Program**
- Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) from 1983
- Developed multiple indigenous missile systems
- Made India self-reliant in missile technology
- Transformed India into a missile power

#### Major Missile Projects

1. **Agni Series** (Ballistic Missiles)
   - Long-range surface-to-surface missiles
   - Nuclear-capable strategic missiles
   - Range: 700 km to 5,000+ km

2. **Prithvi Series** (Tactical Missiles)
   - Short-range ballistic missiles
   - Successfully tested in 1988
   - Multiple variants for different forces

3. **Akash** (Surface-to-Air Missile)
   - Medium-range anti-aircraft defense
   - All-weather capability

4. **Trishul** (Quick Reaction Missile)
   - Short-range surface-to-air missile
   - Rapid response defense system

5. **Nag** (Anti-Tank Missile)
   - Third-generation fire-and-forget missile
   - "Fire and forget" capability

#### Career Highlights

**Scientific Positions:**
- **DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation)**
  - Project Director: SLV-III (India's first satellite launch vehicle)
  - Chief Executive: IGMDP
  
- **ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)**
  - Project Director: India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  - Successfully launched Rohini satellite (1980)

- **Chief Scientific Adviser** to Prime Minister (1992-1999)
- **Principal Scientific Adviser** to Government of India

**Political Career:**
- **11th President of India** (2002-2007)
- Known as the "People's President"
- Most loved and accessible President

#### Major Achievements

1. **Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests (1998)**
   - Chief Project Coordinator
   - Code name: Operation Shakti
   - Made India a nuclear weapons state

2. **Technology Vision 2020**
   - Roadmap for India's development
   - Focus on self-reliance in technology

3. **Inspiration to Youth**
   - Visited schools and colleges extensively
   - Encouraged scientific temper
   - Motivated millions through speeches and books

#### Awards and Honors

- **Bharat Ratna** (1997) - India's highest civilian award
- **Padma Vibhushan** (1990)
- **Padma Bhushan** (1981)
- **Honorary Doctorates**: 48 universities worldwide
- **Von Braun Award**: NASA (2013)

#### Popular Books by Dr. Kalam

1. **"Wings of Fire" (1999)** - Autobiography
2. **"India 2020"** - Vision for developed India
3. **"Ignited Minds"** - Inspiring youth
4. **"My Journey"** - Transforming dreams into actions
5. **"Transcendence"** - Spiritual reflections

#### Personal Life

- **Born**: October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
- **Died**: July 27, 2015, Shillong (while delivering lecture)
- **Education**: Aerospace Engineering (MIT, Madras)
- **Known For**: Simplicity, dedication, and love for students
- **Lifestyle**: Vegetarian, bachelor, deeply spiritual
